# United States v. Microsoft Corporation - Complaint Analysis ## Key Information | Item | Details | |------|---------| | Parties Involved | Plaintiff: United States of America; Defendant: Microsoft Corporation | | Jurisdiction and Venue | U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia | | Case Number | Civil Action No. 98-1232 (Antitrust) | | Date of Document | Filed: May 18, 1998 | | Type of Document | Complaint | | Key Government Officials | Joel I. Klein (Assistant Attorney General), A. Douglas Melamed (Principal Deputy Assistant Attorney General), Rebecca P. Dick (Director of Civil Non-Merger Enforcement), Jeffrey Blattner (Special Counsel for Information Technology) | | Lead Attorneys | Christopher S Crook (Chief), David Boies (Special Trial Counsel) | ## Summary This antitrust complaint alleges that Microsoft has engaged in anticompetitive practices to maintain its monopoly in the PC operating systems market and extend that dominance into the Internet browser market. Key allegations include: 1. Attempting to divide the browser market with Netscape 2. Tying Internet Explorer to the Windows operating system 3. Entering into exclusionary agreements with ISPs, OLSs, ICPs, and OEMs 4. Imposing restrictive licensing terms on OEMs to control PC boot-up and desktop appearance The government argues these actions violate Sections 1 and 2 of the Sherman Act, harming competition, innovation, and consumer choice. ## Causes of Action and Claims 1. Unlawful Exclusive Dealing and Other Exclusionary Agreements (Section 1, Sherman Act) 2. Unlawful Tying (Section 1, Sherman Act) 3. Monopolization of the PC Operating Systems Market (Section 2, Sherman Act) 4. Attempted Monopolization of the Internet Browser Market (Section 2, Sherman Act) ## Damages and Relief Sought 1. Preliminary and permanent injunctions against anticompetitive practices 2. Prohibition of tying Internet Explorer to Windows 3. Prohibition of exclusionary agreements with ISPs, ICPs, and OEMs 4. Restoration of competitive conditions in affected markets 5. Recovery of costs of the action ## Legal Issues 1. Market definition and monopoly power in PC operating systems and browsers 2. Tying of Windows and Internet Explorer 3. Legality of exclusive dealing and other exclusionary agreements 4. Attempted monopolization of the browser market 5. Maintenance of operating system monopoly through anticompetitive means 6. Impact on innovation and consumer welfare 7. Appropriate remedies to address anticompetitive conduct ## Relevant Facts | Date | Event | |------|-------| | Pre-1995 | Microsoft establishes 80%+ market share in PC operating systems | | May 1995 | Microsoft attempts to divide browser market with Netscape | | July 1995 | Microsoft releases Internet Explorer 1.0 | | August 1995 | Microsoft introduces Windows 95 | | 1995-1998 | Microsoft develops IE versions 2.0, 3.0, and 4.0 | | 1996-1998 | Microsoft enters restrictive agreements with ISPs, ICPs, and OEMs | | August 1996 | Microsoft restricts OEMs' ability to modify Windows 95 boot-up | | 1997 | Microsoft integrates IE more deeply into Windows | | Early 1998 | Microsoft's browser share grows from <5% in 1996 to ~50% | | June 1998 | Anticipated release of Windows 98 with integrated IE | ## Relevant Authorities - Sherman Act, Section 1 (15 U.S.C. ยง 1) - Sherman Act, Section 2 (15 U.S.C. ยง 2) - Clayton Act, Section 12 (15 U.S.C. ยง 22) ## Cases and Statutes for Further Research 1.
